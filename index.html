Topics

802.11 PHY evolution; hits and misses

By Srikanth
The WiFi success story has been built on evolving 802.11 standards adding higher speeds and other features. In the PHY evolution, crucial technologies like OFDM, MIMO, higher-order QAM, OFDMA, MU-MIMO etc. have been standardized to move the technology to achieve greater speeds amongst other benefits. In this talk we will talk about what has clicked with respect to practice and some which have not worked well in practice. This will give an insight into the crucial technologies behind the success of WiFi.

Built for Engineers. Driven by AI. Introducing Candela Assist

Candela Assist is an advanced, AI-powered platform designed to streamline wireless testing workflows. It integrates natural language understanding, automation, and visualization tools to empower engineers to design and validate Wi-Fi networks more efficiently.

Enhancing Channel Selection with Adaptive DFS

By Sai Kiran
The objective of this presentation is to provide insight into how adaptive Dynamic Frequency Selection (DFS) can optimize channel selection by minimizing radar interference while maintaining performance in Wi-Fi networks.

Looking closer at the client: A deep dive into STA behavior

By Sainath, Deepika
Access point performance is often the primary focus during Wi-Fi testing, but understanding the client (STA) behavior is critical. This talk explores techniques to simulate and analyze various client-side conditions and reactions.

Unlocking the Lower 6 GHz Band: A Leap Forward for Wi-Fi Innovation in India

By Bhaskar, Yash, Madhan
This presentation will introduce the unlicensed lower 6 GHz spectrum and its critical role in next-generation Wi-Fi. Then we'll examine the current licensed users of this band in India and summarize the main points of India's draft rules for the unlicensed lower 6 GHz spectrum. Next, we'll compare India's plan with those of other countries and discuss the pros and cons of allowing only low-power indoor devices versus higher-power outdoor use. We'll also explain why an Automated Frequency Coordination (AFC) system is essential for managing interference when Standard Power operation is permitted. Finally, we'll offer clear, practical recommendations to enable new unlicensed devices while protecting existing licensed services.

MLO in the Field: Insights into Legacy Devices and Roaming Behavior

By Shymala, Kiranmai, Rajendra
This study evaluates the real-world impact of Multi-Link Operation (MLO) on Wi-Fi roaming performance. A client device will be continuously roamed between two MLO-capable access points for one hour while running active traffic. Throughput, latency, and handoff behavior will be recorded to assess MLO’s ability to maintain performance during frequent transitions. The same test will then be repeated with MLO disabled on the AP. By comparing both scenarios under identical conditions, we aim to determine whether MLO improves roaming efficiency and throughput stability in dynamic enterprise environments.

In-Flight Wi-Fi: Systems, Challenges, and Test Scenarios

By Pranathi, Veena
As passenger expectations for in-flight connectivity grow, delivering stable and high-speed Wi-Fi at cruising altitudes has become both a technical challenge and a critical feature in modern aviation. This presentation begins with an overview of how in-flight connectivity works, tracing the end-to-end data flow between cabin devices, onboard systems, and ground or satellite networks. We dive into the communication protocols involved across flight phases—takeoff, cruise, and landing—with emphasis on handoffs and link transitions. Key physical factors like frequency bands, Doppler shifts, signal propagation at high velocities, and altitude-based constraints are analyzed for their impact on connectivity performance. This session also includes test scenarios validating the Wi-Fi performance in a flight with numerous clients connected at different distances from the access point and analyzes them.

Inside WiFi 8: Exploring the Next Evolution of Wireless Standards

By Amol, Raju
To provide a clear and concise understanding of the upcoming WiFi 8 (IEEE 802.11bn) standard, highlighting its key advancements, performance improvements, and strategic benefits for next generation wireless networks. The presentation will focus on how WiFi 8 will transform connectivity across enterprise, industrial, and consumer applications by delivering higher efficiency, lower latency, and AI/ML-assisted optimization—enabling future-ready use cases like real-time XR, massive IoT, edge cloud, and ultra reliable wireless networking
